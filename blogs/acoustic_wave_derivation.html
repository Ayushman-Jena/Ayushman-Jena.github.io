Derivation of Acoustic Wave Equation in 3D

From Continuity Equation to Nonlinear Westervelt Equation

Published: 2026

1. Introduction

As a part of my ongoing research, I have come accross the aoustic wave equation quite a few times. Since I have been working on High Intensity OFocused Ultrasound for some time now, the major understanding of the non-linearities associated with focused ultrasound is actually related to the way the waves move through the media. Now in order to understand how the wave propagates in general, we must be aware of the linear wave equation as a begining and hpw does it come to be.

1. Governing Equations

The starting point for acoustic wave propagation is the conservation of mass. This is mathematically expressed using the continuity equation, which relates changes in density to the divergence of particle velocity.

Continuity Equation

\[ \frac{\partial \rho}{\partial t} + \nabla \cdot (\rho \mathbf{v}) = 0 \]

Momentum Equation (Euler)

\[ \rho \left( \frac{\partial \mathbf{v}}{\partial t} + (\mathbf{v} \cdot \nabla)\mathbf{v} \right) = -\nabla p \]

Equation of State

\[ p = p(\rho) \]

2. Small Perturbation Approximation

\[ \begin{aligned} p &= p_0 + p' \\ \rho &= \rho_0 + \rho' \\ \mathbf{v} &= \mathbf{v}' \end{aligned} \]

3. Linearization

Continuity

\[ \frac{\partial \rho'}{\partial t} + \rho_0 \nabla \cdot \mathbf{v} = 0 \]

Momentum

\[ \rho_0 \frac{\partial \mathbf{v}}{\partial t} = -\nabla p' \]

Equation of State

\[ p' = c^2 \rho' \]

4. Linear Wave Equation

\[ \nabla^2 p - \frac{1}{c^2} \frac{\partial^2 p}{\partial t^2} = 0 \]

5. Nonlinear Acoustics

\[ p = c^2 \rho + \frac{B}{2A} \frac{\rho^2}{\rho_0} \]

6. Westervelt Equation

\[ \nabla^2 p - \frac{1}{c^2} \frac{\partial^2 p}{\partial t^2} = - \frac{\beta}{\rho_0 c^4} \frac{\partial^2 (p^2)}{\partial t^2} \]

Where:

  • \(\beta = 1 + \frac{B}{2A}\)

7. Including Absorption

\[ \nabla^2 p - \frac{1}{c^2} \frac{\partial^2 p}{\partial t^2} + \delta \frac{\partial}{\partial t} (\nabla^2 p) = - \frac{\beta}{\rho_0 c^4} \frac{\partial^2 (p^2)}{\partial t^2} \]

8. KZK Equation

\[ \frac{\partial p}{\partial z} = \frac{1}{2c} \nabla_\perp^2 p + \frac{\delta}{2c^3} \frac{\partial^2 p}{\partial t^2} + \frac{\beta}{2\rho_0 c^3} \frac{\partial (p^2)}{\partial t} \]

9. Burgers Equation

\[ \frac{\partial p}{\partial x} = \frac{\beta}{\rho_0 c^3} p \frac{\partial p}{\partial t} + \delta \frac{\partial^2 p}{\partial t^2} \]

10. Physical Interpretation

  • Linear: Wave propagation
  • Nonlinear: Harmonic generation
  • Absorption: Energy loss
  • Diffraction: Beam spreading
