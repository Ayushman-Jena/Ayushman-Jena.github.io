COMSOL Simulation of Focused Ultrasound

Ayushman Jena July 2025 8 min read Acoustics COMSOL Simulation

Focused ultrasound is a powerful non-invasive technique used in both medical therapy and material characterization. By concentrating acoustic energy at a specific focal point, it enables targeted heating, ablation, or mechanical disruption of tissue without affecting surrounding structures.

In this work, a spherical cap piezoelectric transducer is modeled and simulated using COMSOL Multiphysics. The primary goal is to characterize the pressure field, focal region geometry, and acoustic intensity distribution produced by a High Intensity Focused Ultrasound (HIFU) transducer operating in water.

Note: This simulation uses the Pressure Acoustics module in COMSOL, solved in the frequency domain. Nonlinear effects are modeled via post-processing using the Westervelt equation formulation.

1. Governing Physics

1.1 Westervelt Equation

The propagation of acoustic waves in a nonlinear medium is governed by the Westervelt equation, which extends the classical wave equation to include second-order nonlinear effects and thermoviscous absorption:

Westervelt Equation
\[ \nabla^2 p - \frac{1}{c_0^2} \frac{\partial^2 p}{\partial t^2} + \frac{\delta}{c_0^4} \frac{\partial^3 p}{\partial t^3} = - \frac{\beta}{\rho_0 c_0^4} \frac{\partial^2 (p^2)}{\partial t^2} \]

where \( p \) is the acoustic pressure, \( c_0 \) is the small-signal speed of sound, \( \rho_0 \) is the ambient density, \( \beta \) is the coefficient of nonlinearity, and \( \delta \) is the sound diffusivity accounting for thermoviscous losses.

The right-hand side term captures nonlinear harmonic generation — the mechanism by which energy transfers from the fundamental frequency to its harmonics as amplitude increases. This is particularly significant in HIFU where peak pressures can exceed several MPa.

1.2 Linear Approximation

For low-amplitude fields or initial modeling, the nonlinear term is neglected, reducing to the Helmholtz equation in the frequency domain:

Helmholtz Equation (Frequency Domain)
\[ \nabla^2 p + k^2 p = 0, \quad k = \frac{\omega}{c_0} \]

The equation of state for the medium relates pressure to density perturbations:

Equation of State
\[ p = c_0^2 \,\rho' \]

2. COMSOL Model Setup

2.1 Geometry

The transducer is modeled as a spherical cap with a fixed radius of curvature \( R = 60 \) mm and aperture diameter \( D = 50 \) mm, giving a geometric focal length of \( f = R \). The surrounding medium is water, characterized by:

  • Speed of sound: \( c_0 = 1500 \) m/s
  • Density: \( \rho_0 = 998 \) kg/m³
  • Nonlinearity parameter: \( \beta = 3.5 \)
  • Operating frequency: \( f_0 = 1 \) MHz

2.2 Boundary Conditions

A normal velocity boundary condition is applied to the transducer surface to simulate uniform piston excitation. A perfectly matched layer (PML) of thickness \( \lambda/4 \) surrounds the computational domain to absorb outgoing waves and prevent artificial reflections:

Transducer BC
\[ \frac{\partial p}{\partial n} = -j \omega \rho_0 \, v_n \]
Mesh: The domain is meshed with quadratic Lagrange elements at a maximum element size of \( \lambda/6 \) to ensure numerical accuracy at 1 MHz. The total mesh contains approximately 180,000 degrees of freedom.

3. Simulation Results

The pressure field computed by COMSOL shows a well-defined focal region at the geometric focal point of the transducer. The peak pressure amplitude at the focus is approximately 12× the source pressure, consistent with the expected focusing gain for these parameters.

The -6 dB focal volume dimensions measured from the simulation are:

  • Axial length: \( \approx 8.2 \) mm
  • Lateral width: \( \approx 1.4 \) mm
  • Aspect ratio: \( \approx 5.9:1 \)

These values agree well with the analytical predictions from O'Neil's formula for a focused piston transducer, validating the simulation setup.

4. Observations

  • Clear focal region formation with strong pressure amplification at the geometric focus
  • Nonlinear harmonic generation visible at higher drive amplitudes — second harmonic amplitude reaches approximately 18% of fundamental at 1 MPa source pressure
  • Beam narrowing due to focusing geometry produces a tight lateral resolution
  • PML boundary effectively absorbs outgoing waves with less than 0.1% reflection
  • Grating lobes absent due to the continuous aperture of the spherical cap geometry
