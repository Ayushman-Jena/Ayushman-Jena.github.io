COMSOL Simulation of Focused Ultrasound

Author: Ayushman Jena | July 2025

Focused ultrasound is a powerful technique used in both medical therapy and material characterization. In this work, a spherical cap piezoelectric transducer is modeled and simulated using COMSOL Multiphysics.

1. Governing Physics

The propagation of acoustic waves in a nonlinear medium is governed by the Westervelt equation.

\[ \nabla^2 p - \frac{1}{c^2} \frac{\partial^2 p}{\partial t^2} = - \frac{\beta}{\rho_0 c^4} \frac{\partial^2 (p^2)}{\partial t^2} \]

This equation captures both linear wave propagation and nonlinear harmonic generation.

2. COMSOL Model Setup

The transducer is modeled as a spherical cap with a fixed radius of curvature. The surrounding medium is water, and pressure acoustics physics is applied in the frequency domain.

\[ p = c^2 \rho \]

3. Simulation Results

The pressure field shows strong focusing at the geometric focal point of the transducer.

4. Observations

  • Clear focal region formation
  • Nonlinear effects visible at higher amplitudes
  • Beam narrowing due to focusing geometry
